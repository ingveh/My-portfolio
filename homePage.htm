Education

I attended primary school and secondary school in my hometown Sandnes. The schools I attended were Austrått skole and Høyland ungdomsskole respectively. Here I began learning French and was particularly fond of mathematics and natural sciences. I even participated in a group for pupils with strong mathematical skills.

After secondary school I continued on to Stavanger katedralskole, an upper secondary school in Stavanger. Here I continued learning French, pursued the science subjects mathematics, physics and chemistry, and most importantly I attended the school via a programme for pupils which were both skilled and had an above average interest in science. This meant that during my attendance at the school, I took additional subjects such as extra mathematics and Python programming. I also competed in a competition issued by the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) where we measured and analysed environmental conditions around a lake using a self made sensor package. Lastly, during my last year at Stavanger Katedralskole I had opportunity to take a university level physics course in mechanics at the University of Stavanger (UiS) in parallel with my normal subjects.

Next I went on to study for a bachelor's degree in Physics at NTNU. I have currently finished three years of the degree and have written a thesis on how an electron behaves quantum mechanically in a system of interest. The subject I have enjoyed the most is quantum mechanics, and in addition to my physics subjects I immensely enjoy mathematics and programming. I have learned and used Python programming a lot for projects and laboratory work during my degree, and it is thus my main programming language. However, I am planning on attending a course in C++ programminng, and I am currently teaching myself web design using HTML, CSS and JavaScript.
